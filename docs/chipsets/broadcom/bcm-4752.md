## Broadcom GNSS Chipsets

### BCM4752

The BCM4752 is Broadcom's third-generation multi-constellation GNSS receiver, supporting GPS + GLONASS + QZSS.

It was announced on 21 Mar 2012 in [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2012/3/21/2890127/broadcom-gps-chip-indoor-positioning-bcm4752), then in Apr 2012 by [Azertac](https://azertag.az/en/xeber/broadcom_introduces_new_gps_chip_offering_a_platform_for_development_of_indoor_positioning_applications-77267) and [Coordinates](https://mycoordinates.org/gnss-receiver-covers-all-multi-constellation-standards/).

The BCM4752 appears in several Android phones, tablets, and watches:

- [Samsung Galaxy S4 (Exynos)](https://www.techinsights.com/blog/samsung-galaxy-s4-teardown) - Apr 2013
- [Samsung Galaxy S5 Mini (Exynos)](https://www.ifixit.com/Teardown/Samsung+Galaxy+S5+Mini+Teardown/27579) - Jul 2014
- [Samsung Gear Sport](https://gethypoxic.com/blogs/technical/samsung-gear-sport-teardown) - Oct 2017
- Samsung Galaxy Tab Active 2 - Nov 2017
